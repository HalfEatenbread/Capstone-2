About Tetris




Short History of Tetris

Tetris is a classic puzzle video game that has captivated players since its inception. Here’s a brief history of Tetris , from its creation to the present day:



1. Creation (1984-1985):


- Tetris was created by Alexey Pajitnov, a Soviet software engineer, while he was working at the Dorodnitsyn Computing Centre of the Soviet Academy of Sciences in Moscow.
- The first version was developed on an Electronika 60 computer and was inspired by a puzzle game called Pentominoes.



2. Initial Release (1985):


- The game was officially released on June 6, 1985, and quickly gained popularity within the Soviet Union.



3. International Spread (1986-1989):


- Tetris made its way to the West in the late 1980s, thanks to various software developers who ported it to different platforms, including the IBM PC and the Commodore 64.
- The game’s addictive nature led to its widespread acclaim and numerous adaptations.



4. Game Boy Launch (1989):


- The release of the Nintendo Game Boy in 1989 included Tetris as a pack-in game, which significantly boosted its popularity and introduced it to a broader audience.
- This version is often credited with helping to establish the Game Boy as a successful handheld console.



5. Legal Battles (1990s):


- The success of Tetris led to various legal disputes over licensing rights, particularly between Pajitnov and several companies that had developed versions of the game.
- In 1996, Pajitnov finally gained the rights to Tetris, and he co-founded The Tetris Company to manage the game's licensing.



6. Modern Era (2000s-Present):


- Tetris has continued to evolve with technology, appearing on numerous platforms, including mobile devices, consoles, and PCs.
- The game has seen various iterations, including Tetris 99, a battle royale version released in 2019, which has been well-received by both new and veteran players.
- Tetris remains a cultural icon, celebrated for its simple yet challenging gameplay, and it continues to be featured in competitive gaming events.



7. Current Developments:


- As of 2024, Tetris is still actively developed and updated, with new versions and adaptations being released regularly, ensuring that it remains relevant in the gaming community.



Tetris has not only stood the test of time but has also influenced countless other games and remains a beloved classic in the world of video gaming.




List of Tetris variants:

There are multiple Tetris variants over the course of Tetris' history, both officially licensed and unofficial clones. Here is an overview of some notable Tetris variants:



Officially Licensed Tetris Games:

  • Tetris (1984) - The original game that started it all.
  • Tetris 2 (1990) - Introduced new gameplay mechanics and modes.
  • Tetris Attack (1996) - A puzzle game that uses Tetris mechanics but with a different gameplay style.
  • Tetris Worlds (2001) - Featured multiple game modes and online play.
  • Tetris DS (2006) - Included various modes and multiplayer options.
  • Tetris Effect (2018) - A visually stunning version that combines music and visuals with classic Tetris gameplay.

Unofficial clones and variants:

  • Tetris 3D - A three-dimensional take on the classic game.
  • Torus - A variant that allows pieces to wrap around the edges of the playfield.
  • Tetris Blitz - A fast-paced, time-limited version of Tetris.
  • Tetr.io - An online multiplayer version with competitive features.
  • Puyo Puyo Tetris - A crossover that combines Tetris with the Puyo Puyo series.

Unique variants:

  • Tetris 99 - A battle royale version where 99 players compete against each other.
  • Tetris Friends - A platform that offered various Tetris games online.
  • Tetrimino - A variant that allows for different shapes and sizes of pieces

Alternative Versions:

  • Blockout - A 3D version of Tetris that adds depth to the gameplay.
  • Tetris Party - A party game version with various mini-games and multiplayer options.



Recent Achievements involving Tetris games

Over these few years, the Tetris community has witnessed a remarkable array of world records and achievements that highlight the skill and dedication of its players. Here is an overview of some of the milestones:



Recent World Records:

  • Alex Thach, a young prodigy, has made headlines by breaking every Tetris world record. In recent competitions,he achieved a staggering score of 16,700,760 million points in a modified game cartridge of Tetris, solidifying his status as one of the top players in the world.
  • The Classic Tetris World Championship has continued to be a premier event for Tetris enthusiasts. In the past few years, players have consistently pushed the boundaries of high scores. Seven people have held the title as World Champion: Jonas Neubauer, Harry Hong, Joseph Saelee, dogplayingtetris, EricICX, Fractal and the reigning 2024 winner Alex T, with Jonas, Joseph and dog holding multiple titles. Eight more have contested them as runners-up: Alex Kerr, Mike Winzinek, Quaid, Jeff Moore, Koryan, PixelAndy, Huffulufugus and Sidnev , with Alex being the only to contest the championship finals twice.
  • Willis Gibson, a 13-year-old from Oklahoma, has made history by becoming the first person believed to have ever completed the original Nintendo version of Tetris. This remarkable achievement comes nearly 40 years after the game's release, a feat that many players thought was impossible. Gibson's record-setting playthrough lasted around 39 minutes, during which he reached an unprecedented level of 157. This level is particularly significant because it is known to cause the game to crash, highlighting the extraordinary skill and precision required to achieve it.
